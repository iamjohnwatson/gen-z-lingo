🔥 gen z slang and vibes ⚡

by Akash Sriram, a confused millennial

😎 💯 🔥 👀 💅 ✨

Okay, I’m Akash, a 29-year-old millennial, and I’m totally lost with Gen Z slang. Like, what even is “skibidi”? I thought I was hip with “lit,” but apparently, there’s a whole new language out there! So, I made this project to figure out Gen Z lingo, help other confused millennials like me, and maybe bridge our generational gap. Let’s dive into this wild world together.

Gen Z slang is everywhere, born from the internet—think TikTok, Twitter, and memes. Words like “stan” come from Eminem’s 2000 song about an obsessive fan, but now it means you’re a superfan. I stan coffee, I guess? Then there’s “yeet,” which started in a 2014 viral video of a kid yelling it while throwing something. Now it’s an all-purpose hype word. I tried saying “yeet” at work, and people just stared at me.

Some terms, like “flex,” have roots in 90s hip-hop but got a Gen Z glow-up. It means showing off—like, “He’s flexing his new car.” I’m still figuring out “no cap” (it means “honest,” apparently), and don’t get me started on “vibe check.” Gen Z pulls a lot from African American Vernacular English (AAVE) and internet culture, remixing language faster than I can keep up. It’s chaotic, but kind of amazing how they use slang to connect and express authenticity.

I’m baffled, but I’m learning! Gen Z’s creativity is next-level, and their slang reflects a generation that’s all about community and humor. If I can get it, so can you—let’s explore this together!
